Motivation Barometer & THRIVE

About this dataset

The Motivation Barometer & THRIVE dataset brings together repeated follow-up surveys with a large panel of participants in Belgium. It follows people’s motivation, psychological functioning, and societal attitudes during and after the COVID-19 crisis and across broader societal challenges.

Total overview of variables

1. Core psychological processes

  • Basic Psychological Needs
    • Autonomy (satisfaction and frustration)
    • Competence (satisfaction and frustration)
    • Relatedness (satisfaction and frustration)
  • Motivation
    • Adherence to the measures
      • Identified motivation
      • Introjected motivation
      • External motivation
      • Amotivation
      • Effort
      • Reactance
    • Vaccination
      • Identified motivation
      • External motivation
      • Amotivation (defiance)
      • Amotivation (distrust)
      • Amotivation (self-efficacy)
    • Measures-specific: face covering, hand washing, taking pcr test, physical distance, quarantaine, social distancing, ventilation
    • Outcome expectation: physical distance, hand washing, face covering, pcr test, social contact, telework, ventilatino (self and population)
    • Self-efficacy: physical distance, hand washing, face covering, pcr test, social contact, telework, ventilatino (duration and effort)
  • Well-being and Ill-being
    • Anxiety
    • Aspiration (importance and in practice, post-crisis)
    • Boredom
    • Climate anxiety
    • Concerns: health, significant others, situation, finances, job, climate change, war, new COVID-19 variants
    • Depression
    • Emotion-Regulation
    • Life satisfaction
    • Loneliness
    • Mindfulness
    • Novelty
    • Parental stress
    • Sleep duration
    • Vitality and depletion

2. Behavior and behavioral intentions

  • Behavior
    • adherence to the measures (keeping physical distance, hand washing, avoiding social contect, wearing mouthmask, working from home, ventilation)
    • planned adherence
    • taking part in a protest
  • Intention to climate activism

3. Perceptions of crisis and society

  • Risk perception

    • COVID-19: perceived infection chance and severity of symptoms (towards self and population)
    • future crises: perceived infection chance and severity of symptoms (towards self and population)

  • Trust

    • how competence one is perceived to be (celebrities, doctors, experts, nurses, healthcare, pharmacists, politicans)
    • trust and perceived expertise with doctor, expert, media, pharmacist, politicians, other vaccinated persons

  • Societal themes

    • populism
    • anomie
    • autocratic versus democratic attitudes

  • Perception of how the crisis is handled by the policy

  • Evaluation of: the measures, obligated vaccination

  • Memories of the COVID-19 crisis

  • Future crisis

    • approach
    • preparedness

  • Conspiracy theories

    • On corona
    • General

  • Communication style

    • Autonomy-supportive (Participative, Attuning)
    • Structure (Guiding, Clarifying)
    • Controlling (Demanding, Domineering)
    • Chaos (Abandoning, Awaiting)

4. Contextual and background variables

  • Meta
    • Date
    • Weather (daily temperature, sun duration, humidity)
    • Google Mobility data
    • Holiday periods
    • Season
  • Sociodemographics
    • age
    • civil status
    • comorbidities
    • infection/hospitalization status
    • education level
    • financial situation
    • gender
    • language
    • physical activity
    • province of living
    • religion
    • socio-economic status
    • work situation
  • Crisis-related information
    • Infections: raw numbers, incremental value (source: Sciensano)
    • Hospitalizations: raw numbers, incremental value (source: Sciensano)
    • Mortalities: raw numbers, incremental value (source: Sciensano)
    • Number of vaccination doses (A - EEE, source: Sciensano)
    • Stringency Index (source: Oxford Govermental Tool)

Variables by year

2025: Post-crisis adjustment & regimes

  • Basic psychological needs

    Perceived support and thwart in life

  • Well-being and ill-being

    The development of a circumplex pattern

  • Societal attitudes

  • Old and new crises

    Risk perception COVID-19 and climate crisis
    Intented usage of COVID-19 hygiene behaviors at work and at family occassions when feeling sick

2024: Post-crisis adjustment & regimes

  • Basic psychological needs (personal & group-based)
    Autonomy, competence, and relatedness satisfaction and frustration; group-based needs.

  • Well-being and ill-being
    Anxiety, depression, depletion, vitality, life satisfaction, sleep quality, concerns, loneliness, flourishing/mental health (MHC), novelty, self-compassion, post-crisis and post-lockdown perceptions.

  • Perceptions of the COVID-19 crisis & future crises
    Memories of the crisis, post-crisis perception, approach to future crises, risk perception of future crises.

  • Democratic attitudes and societal views
    Perceptions of democracy and autocracy, anomie, populist attitudes, conspiracy beliefs (incl. corona), views on experts and government.

  • Information sources & communication
    Use of information sources, following governmental communication (e.g., press conferences).

  • Social identities & group membership
    Identity centrality, national and government identification, social group variables.

  • Sociodemographics
    Age, gender, education, work situation, socio-economic and financial situation, province, nationality, number of children, partner status, political orientation, language, consent, and survey metadata.

2022: COVID transition period, climate concern & vaccination attitudes

  • Basic psychological needs
    Satisfaction and frustration of autonomy, competence, relatdness

  • Well-being and ill-being
    Anxiety, depression, depletion
    Vitality & life satisfaction
    Sleep quality & sleep duration
    Concerns and irritations (e.g., annoyance by others)

  • Crisis perceptions & societal attitudes
    Attitudes toward obligatory vaccination
    Perceived risk (general)
    Climate anxiety and climate-related worry
    Intention toward climate activism

  • Vaccination behaviour, intentions & motivation
    Vaccination status (dose count: 1–4 doses, one/two doses)
    Intention for yearly booster
    Beliefs about vaccine efficacy & safety
    Motivation for vaccination (SDT-based): External motivation, Identified motivation, Amotivation (defiance, distrust, self-efficacy)

  • Perceptions of experts, government & institutions
    Trust in experts and government
    Perceived competence, integrity, benevolence of authorities

  • Behaviour when symptomatic
    Protective behaviour and adherence
    (e.g., avoiding interactions, mask use, informing others)

  • Sociodemographics & personal background
    Age, gender, education, region/province, work situation, partner status, number of children, Belgian region of living, religion, socio-economic status & financial situation, comorbidities, recent infection (COVID-19)
