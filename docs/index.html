PsySoc Data Hub

Welcome to the PsySoc Data Hub: the Psychology & Society Data Hub. This platform serves as a central repository for large-scale datasets on themes regarding psychology and society.

The name PsySoc highlights the mission of the Data Hub: to bridge psychological science with societal topics by fostering collaboration across research domains, practitioners, and policy actors, an essential endeavour both in times of crisis and for society‚Äôs long-term resilience.

Current datasets

Motivation Barometer & THRIVE Datasets

At the heart of the hub lies the Motivation Barometer, a landmark monitoring project that followed the psychological functioning of the Belgian population from the earliest days of the COVID-19 crisis. Across more than 110 waves and over 320,000 participants, the project tracked motivation, psychological needs, risk perception, adherence, well-being, and trust.

The THRIVE project builds on this infrastructure, extending it beyond the pandemic with new longitudinal waves and additional qualitative and quantitative data. Together, these efforts create a rich, theory-driven resource for studying resilience, vulnerability, democratic attitudes, communication, and long-term recovery.

Both datasets are integrated into one cohesive data ecosystem, allowing researchers to seamlessly explore trends over time, compare modules, and link psychological indicators to societal developments.

Louvain Foundation Dataset

The Louvain Foundation dataset follows more than 5000 UCLouvain students and Belgian residents across seven waves from 2021‚Äì2022, with a follow-up wave in 2025. It gives many information into COVID-19‚Äìrelated perceptions and behaviors (e.g., trust in health authorities, adherence to public health measures), alongside well-being, social connection, emotional responses, and other psychological indicators during and after the pandemic. Fully anonymized by design, it offers a secure and useful resource for research on the social and psychological impact of COVID-19.

üìù Layer 2: Registered Access

Restricted, de-identified versions of the real data are available on request.

To apply, researchers provide:

  1. A brief project description
  2. Ethical approval (if applicable)
  3. Required variables/modules

Approved users sign a Data Use Agreement, committing to:

  • No re-identification
  • No redistribution
  • Secure storage
  • Proper citation

Access is granted via private links tied to a specific dataset version.

Versioning and Reproducibility

To ensure transparency and reproducibility, all datasets are:

  • Released in clearly labeled versions (e.g., v1.0, v1.1)
  • Accompanied by release notes describing changes in variables, coding, or sample composition
  • Linked to DOIs for persistent citation

Analysis scripts and Quarto documents are aligned with specific data versions, allowing users to reproduce published work and tutorial examples exactly.
